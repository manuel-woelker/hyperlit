# Metcalfe's Law

**Statement:** The value of a telecommunications network is proportional to the square of the number of connected users of the system (nÂ²).

**Proposed by:** Robert Metcalfe (1980s)

**Key Insight:** Network effects cause the value of a network to increase exponentially as more users join.

**Implications:**
- Explains the rapid growth of social networks and platforms
- First-mover advantage is significant in network-based businesses
- Critical mass is essential for network-based services to succeed
- Helps explain winner-takes-all dynamics in platform markets
